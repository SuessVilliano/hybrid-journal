# Part I — The Power of Journaling

## Chapter 1: Why a Trading Journal Changes Everything

Most traders fail for the same reason: they have no memory. They take a trade, win or lose, feel something about it, and move on. The lesson evaporates. Six weeks later they make the identical mistake — same setup, same emotion, same result — and never connect the dots.

A journal is the cure. It converts scattered experience into a searchable, measurable record you can actually learn from. The number on your account is the *result*. The journal is the *process* that produced it — and process is the only thing you can improve.

**The hard truth about trading performance:**

- Your edge is not your strategy. It's your *consistency in executing* your strategy.
- You don't lose money on bad setups. You lose money on good setups you traded badly — sized wrong, entered early, exited in fear.
- The gap between a 45% win rate and a 55% win rate is almost never knowledge. It's behavior. Behavior is invisible until you write it down.

Hybrid Journal exists to make that behavior visible. Every page in the app — the planner, the trade log, the journal, the analytics, the AI coach — is built around one loop:

> **Decide on purpose → Record what actually happened → Compare → Adjust.**

This is the difference between *ten years of experience* and *one year of experience repeated ten times*.

### What you are really journaling

You are not just logging prices. You're capturing four layers:

| Layer | Question it answers | Where it lives in the app |
|---|---|---|
| **The numbers** | What did the trade do? | Trade log: entry, exit, P&L, R:R |
| **The reasoning** | Why did I take it? | Trade Setup & Notes fields |
| **The emotion** | What state was I in? | Emotion Before/After, Journal moods |
| **The intention** | Did I plan this? | Daily Plan link & alignment score |

When all four are recorded, patterns surface that no broker statement could ever show you — like the fact that your "Anxious" trades lose money 70% of the time, or that every trade you took *without* a plan was a loser.

## Chapter 2: The Hybrid Method — Plan → Execute → Journal → Review

Hybrid Journal is built on a four-stage daily loop. The whole app is organized to support it.

```
   ┌─────────────┐      ┌─────────────┐      ┌─────────────┐      ┌─────────────┐
   │  1. PLAN    │ ───▶ │ 2. EXECUTE  │ ───▶ │ 3. JOURNAL  │ ───▶ │ 4. REVIEW   │
   │             │      │             │      │             │      │             │
   │ Daily Plan  │      │  Log each   │      │ Capture the │      │ AI Coach +  │
   │ Setup grades│      │   trade     │      │  emotion &  │      │ Analytics + │
   │ Risk limits │      │ as it closes│      │  the lesson │      │  Summaries  │
   └─────────────┘      └─────────────┘      └─────────────┘      └─────────────┘
          ▲                                                              │
          └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
                        Tomorrow's plan is shaped by today's review
```

**Stage 1 — Plan (before the market opens).** On the **Trade Plans** page you write a Daily Plan: the markets you'll watch, your A/B/C setup grades, your max trades, your max risk, and your rules. This is your contract with yourself.

**Stage 2 — Execute (during the session).** On the **Trades** page you log each position. The form captures price data *and* psychology — your emotion before and after, the setup, your reasoning.

**Stage 3 — Journal (after the session).** On the **Journal** page you write freely about how the day felt. Not numbers — *state of mind*. Tag your moods. Let the AI read your sentiment.

**Stage 4 — Review (daily, weekly, monthly).** The **AI Coach**, **Analytics**, and **Summaries** pages turn your records into feedback. The plan's *alignment score* tells you whether you actually did what you said you'd do.

A trader who closes this loop every day for a quarter is unrecognizable from the one who started it. That is the entire promise of this guidebook.

## Chapter 5: Accounts, Brokers & Importing Trade History

Before you journal new trades, bring in your history. A journal with 6 months of past trades gives the AI and analytics something real to chew on from day one.

### Set up a trading account

Go to **Accounts** → *My Accounts* → add an account. Fields:

- **Name** (e.g. "FTMO Challenge #2"), **Broker**, **Account number**
- **Type** — Live, Demo, Prop Firm, or Paper
- **Currency**, **Initial balance**, **Active** status

If you choose **Prop Firm** as the type, the app opens **Prop Firm Settings** so you can record firm name, account size, max daily loss %, max total loss %, trailing drawdown %, profit target %, the phase (Challenge / Verification / Funded), and whether weekend holding and news trading are allowed. These numbers later power live rule-tracking on your dashboard.

> **Tip — multi-account:** Free supports 1 account, Pro supports 3, Team is unlimited. The Dashboard has a **Global Account Selector** so you can view stats for one account or all of them combined.

### Three ways to get trades in

| Method | Best for | How |
|---|---|---|
| **Manual entry** | New trades, discretionary traders | Trades page → *Add Trade* |
| **Statement import** | Backfilling history | Imports page → upload CSV/PDF |
| **Broker auto-sync** | Hands-off ongoing logging | Accounts → Broker Connections (3-step wizard) |

### Importing a statement (Imports page)

Upload your broker export. Supported formats include **MetaTrader 4/5** (HTML, CSV), **cTrader** (CSV), **DXTrade** (CSV, PDF), **MatchTrader** (CSV), **Rithmic** (CSV), **TradingView** (CSV), **Tradovate** (CSV), and a **generic CSV**.

For a generic CSV, the only strictly required columns are **`symbol`** and **`pnl`** (or `profit`). Strongly recommended: `entry_date`, `entry_price`, `exit_price`, `side`, `quantity`. Optional: `stop_loss`, `take_profit`, `commission`, `swap`, `strategy`, `notes`.

PDF statements are read by AI and take roughly 30–60 seconds. Each import becomes a tracked record (Pending → Processing → Completed / Failed) you can rename, analyze, or delete.

> **No duplicates, ever.** Hybrid Journal deduplicates on `source + source_trade_id`. If a statement you upload contains trades that were already auto-synced, they're detected and skipped or updated — never doubled.

### Broker auto-sync

**Accounts → Broker Connections → Connect Broker** runs a 3-step wizard: pick the platform, choose the connection method (**API** real-time, **Webhook Push** for MT4/MT5 EAs, or **Statement Ingest**), and enter credentials with live validation. Once connected, new trades flow into your journal automatically.

# Part III — The Daily Practice

This is the heart of the book. The previous chapters were setup; these three chapters are the habit that actually makes you money.

## Chapter 7: Morning — Build Your Daily Plan

Before the market opens, go to **Trade Plans** and create today's **Daily Plan**. The app's own research note says it plainly: *traders who plan their day are 3× more likely to follow their rules.*

A Daily Plan in Hybrid Journal can capture:

- **Plan text** — your written narrative for the day (you can also dictate it; the app keeps a voice transcript)
- **Pre-market checklist** — tickable items (e.g. "Checked economic calendar", "Reviewed higher timeframe", "Slept 7+ hours")
- **Trading rules** — the specific rules you commit to today
- **Markets to watch** — your shortlist of instruments
- **Setup grades** — define **A**, **B**, **C** setups with descriptions and criteria. (These later appear as a dropdown in the trade form, so you grade every trade against your *own* standard.)
- **Risk limits** — max trades, max risk, max risk % per trade, max daily loss / %, trailing drawdown %
- **Trade targets** — planned entry, stop loss, take profit; the app can show calculated position size and monetary risk
- **Chart screenshots** — upload your pre-market levels
- **Links** — attach the strategies and goals this day serves; trades you take get linked back automatically

When you save, the AI coach gives the plan a **Clarity Score (0–100)** plus a short summary and suggestions. A vague plan scores low; a specific, measurable one scores high. Aim for 80+.

### Worked example — a Daily Plan

> **Date:** Mon — **Markets to watch:** NQ, ES, EURUSD
> **Plan text:** "CPI tomorrow, not today — expect rangebound chop. Trading the US open only (9:30–11:00 ET), then done. Bias neutral; I will only trade A-setups."
> **Checklist:** ☑ Economic calendar checked ☑ Higher-timeframe levels marked ☑ Slept well ☑ Phone on Do Not Disturb
> **Setup grades:**
> - **A** — Opening-range breakout with volume confirmation, retest holds
> - **B** — Trend pullback to 9-EMA in first hour
> - **C** — Counter-trend scalp (avoid today)
> **Rules:** (1) Max 3 trades. (2) A-setups only. (3) Stop trading at 11:00 ET or after 2 losses. (4) 0.5% risk per trade.
> **Risk limits:** Max trades 3 · Max risk/trade 0.5% · Max daily loss 1.5%
> **Targets (primary idea):** NQ long over opening range high, stop below range low, TP at 2R.

That plan will score high on Clarity because every line is specific and checkable. Tomorrow, the app will measure your trades against it.

## Chapter 8: In-Session — Log Every Trade

Every position goes on the **Trades** page via **Add Trade**. Log it *as it closes*, while the reasoning and emotion are still fresh — not from memory at night.

### The trade form, field by field

**Identify it:**
- **Symbol** (required) — type `EURUSD`, `NQ`, `BTC/USD`. The app **auto-detects the instrument type**.
- **Platform** — DXTrade, cTrader, MatchTrader, Rithmic, MT4, MT5, Tradovate, NinjaTrader, TradingView, Binance, Other
- **Instrument Type** — Forex, Futures, Stocks, Options, Crypto, CFD
- **Side** — Long or Short

**Price & size:**
- Entry/Exit **date & time**, Entry/Exit **price**, **Quantity/Lots**
- **P&L** (required) — *leave it blank and the app calculates it* from entry, exit, quantity, side, commission and swap
- **Stop Loss**, **Take Profit**, **Commission**, **Swap**

**Strategy & psychology — this is the part that makes it a journal:**
- **Strategy** — e.g. Breakout, Scalping, Swing
- **Setup Grade** — if you defined A/B/C grades in today's plan, grade this trade here
- **Emotion Before** — Confident, Anxious, Calm, Excited, Fearful, Impatient, Disciplined, Impulsive
- **Emotion After** (appears once an exit price exists) — Confident, Anxious, Calm, Excited, Satisfied, Regretful, Disciplined
- **Trade Setup** — describe the conditions: *"What indicators aligned? What pattern did you see?"*
- **Notes** — your reasoning: *"What went well? What could be improved?"*
- **Followed rules?** — the honesty checkbox that powers your alignment score

**Extras:**
- **Attachments** — upload chart screenshots (images) and PDFs
- **Custom Fields** — add your own data points (e.g. "Market Session = London", "VIX = 18")
- **Tags** — free-form labels like `mistake`, `good-entry`, `news-trade`, `revenge-trade`, `A+`

### The AI Auto-Fill button

Enter at least a Symbol and Entry Price, then hit **Auto-Fill with AI**. The assistant suggests entry/exit reasons, runs sentiment analysis on your notes, proposes a trade category (Breakout, Reversal, Continuation, Scalp, Swing, Other), and adds suggested tags. Treat its output as a draft — *edit it to the truth*.

### Trade templates

When you click *Add Trade*, you first pick a **template** — Scalping, Day Trading, Swing Trading, Options, Futures, Crypto, or Forex. Templates pre-fill sensible defaults and can carry their own analysis prompts, including a **Pre-Trade Plan** field and a **Post-Trade Review** field. For prop-firm and swing traders especially, the Pre-Trade Plan / Post-Trade Review pair is gold.

### Worked example — two trades, same day

**Trade 1 — the disciplined one (tag it `A+`):**
> EURUSD · Long · Forex · entry 1.0840 → exit 1.0875 · qty 1.0 · P&L +$350
> SL 1.0820 · TP 1.0880 · Strategy: Breakout · Setup Grade: **A**
> Emotion Before: **Calm** · Emotion After: **Satisfied** · Followed rules: ✅
> Trade Setup: "London open. Price broke the Asian range high on rising volume, retested cleanly. My A-setup exactly."
> Notes: "Entered on the retest, not the break — no chasing. Sized 0.5% risk. Took 1.75R, slightly short of TP because momentum stalled into NY open. Good decision."
> Tags: `A+`, `breakout`, `disciplined`

**Trade 2 — the lesson (tag it `mistake`):**
> NQ · Long · Futures · entry 18420 → exit 18388 · qty 2 · P&L −$256
> Strategy: "none, really" · Setup Grade: **C** · Emotion Before: **Impatient** · Emotion After: **Regretful** · Followed rules: ❌
> Trade Setup: "No setup. I was flat and bored 40 minutes after my one good trade."
> Notes: "This is a revenge-of-boredom trade. It wasn't on my plan, it was a C-grade at best, and I was Impatient before clicking. I broke rule #2 (A-setups only) and rule #1 was already met. The loss is small; the *pattern* is the danger."
> Tags: `mistake`, `revenge-trade`, `unplanned`, `boredom`

Notice what Trade 2 does: it doesn't hide the loss, it *names the behavior*. Three months from now, the AI coach and the analytics will be able to count exactly how many `boredom` / `unplanned` trades you took and what they cost — because you tagged them honestly today.

## Chapter 9: Evening — Write the Journal

The **Journal** page is different from the trade log. The trade log records *trades*; the Journal records *you*. As the app says: *"Trading is 90% mental."*

### How to write an entry

Click **New Journal Entry**, then:
1. Write — or **speak** — freely in the text box. (The mic button does live voice-to-text in Chrome/Edge.) Prompt yourself: *how you felt, an insight, a mistake, a lesson learned.*
2. Tag your **moods** from 15 options: Confident, Anxious, Excited, Fearful, Calm, Frustrated, Disciplined, Impulsive, Focused, Distracted, Motivated, Tired, Stressed, Optimistic, Doubtful.
3. Optionally hit **Analyze Sentiment** — the AI tags the entry positive / negative / neutral / mixed.
4. **Save.**

On each saved entry card you can hit the **Sparkles** icon to have an AI trading-psychology coach add 1–2 sentences of constructive insight. Entries are grouped by day, searchable, and filterable by mood.

### What to actually write — three reliable prompts

You don't need to write an essay. Three or four honest sentences against these prompts beats a blank page every time:

1. **State** — "What was my mental and physical state today, and where did it come from?"
2. **Process** — "Did I follow my plan? Where exactly did I deviate, and what triggered it?"
3. **Lesson** — "If I could re-trade today, what is the *one* thing I'd change tomorrow?"

### Worked example — an evening journal entry

> **Moods tagged:** Disciplined, Frustrated, Focused
>
> "Solid day on process, mixed on emotion. The EURUSD A-setup was textbook and I traded it perfectly — Calm going in, no chasing. I'm proud of that.
>
> The NQ trade is the story. I'd already met my one good trade and my plan said *A-setups only, max 3 trades.* Forty minutes of nothing made me Impatient and I clicked a C-grade long out of boredom. Lost $256 — small money, but it's the third 'boredom trade' I can remember this month. The frustration I feel now isn't about the loss, it's about knowing better and doing it anyway.
>
> **Lesson for tomorrow:** when I've taken my planned trade and nothing else qualifies, I will physically close the platform until the next session window. Boredom is a setup — for losing. Tomorrow I add a checklist item: *'Walk away after first A-setup if no B+ present.'*"

That entry, tagged and saved, becomes data. Run **Mood Correlation** in the AI Coach next month and you may well find your "Impatient"/"Frustrated" days are your worst — a fact you can now *act* on.

> **Streak tip:** journaling daily also builds your activity streak and earns XP (see Chapter 16). The habit compounds — both literally in your account and in the gamification system.

## Chapter 10: Zooming Out — Weekly & Monthly Planning

Daily plans keep you disciplined inside a session. **Weekly** and **Monthly** plans keep your *days* pointed in the same direction. All three live under **Trade Plans** (with deeper views under Strategic Planning).

### Weekly Plan

Set before the week starts:
- **Week start date** and **Market bias** — Bullish, Bearish, Neutral, or Mixed
- **Plan text** — key themes and opportunities
- **Instruments to focus** on and **primary setups** you'll hunt
- **Economic events** on the calendar
- **Weekly goals** — max trades, profit target, max risk, win-rate target
- **Rules to follow** and **things to avoid**
- Optional chart screenshots; the AI generates a summary

A weekly plan can be linked to its parent **Monthly Plan**, and it tracks execution (on-track flag, profit-so-far, pivot notes).

### Monthly Plan

The big picture:
- **Big-picture thesis** — your macro market outlook
- **Strategy focus** and **instruments universe** for the month
- **Challenges to overcome** (pulled from your honest self-assessment)
- **Monthly goals** — profit target, max drawdown, min win rate, max trades, consistency target, skill-development goals
- **Risk parameters** — max daily loss, max weekly loss, max position size, risk per trade
- **Learning objectives**

### The point: alignment

Plans nest: **Monthly → Weekly → Daily → Trades.** The **Plan Correlation View** shows that whole chain and computes an **Overall Alignment Score (0–100)** — how well your actual trading matched your stated intentions, top to bottom. It also flags "On Track" / "Off Track" at each level and surfaces *pivot warnings* when you've drifted.

This is the most powerful accountability tool in the app. A profitable month with a *low* alignment score is a warning, not a victory — it means you got lucky, not good. A high alignment score with a small profit is a *better* month, because the process is repeatable.

# Part IV — Learning From Your Data

## Chapter 11: Analytics & the Hybrid Score™

Logging is input. **Analytics** is where it pays off. The Analytics page has four tabs:

- **AI Insights** — pattern recognition: which trades are profitable, risk prediction, emotional-impact analysis, strategy recommendations
- **Comprehensive Stats** — breakdowns by platform, by instrument type, by long/short, plus best and worst symbols
- **Detailed Charts** — distributions, drawdown, correlations
- **Trade History** — the full sortable, filterable list

### Metrics that matter

| Metric | Formula | What it tells you |
|---|---|---|
| **Win Rate** | wins ÷ total × 100 | How often you're right |
| **Profit Factor** | gross profit ÷ gross loss | Every $1 lost returns $X. >1 is profitable; aim >1.5 |
| **Avg Win / Avg Loss** | mean P&L of winners / losers | Your reward-to-risk in practice |
| **Equity Curve** | running cumulative P&L | Smooth and rising = healthy; jagged = inconsistent |

> **Win rate is overrated on its own.** A 40% win rate with a 3:1 reward/risk crushes a 65% win rate with a 1:2. Always read win rate *next to* profit factor and avg win/avg loss.

### The Hybrid Score™

Your **Hybrid Score** (a widget on the Dashboard) is a single 0–15 rating of your trading health, built from three 5-point components:

**1. Win Rate (0–5):** 70%+ = 5 · 60%+ = 4 · 50%+ = 3 · 40%+ = 2

**2. Profit Factor (0–5):** 3.0+ = 5 · 2.0+ = 4 · 1.5+ = 3 · 1.0+ = 2

**3. Consistency (0–5):** 8+ consecutive profitable weeks = 5 · 6+ = 4 · 4+ = 3 · 2+ = 2

**Your tier:**

| Score | Tier |
|---|---|
| 13–15 | Expert |
| 10–12 | Advanced |
| 7–9 | Intermediate |
| 4–6 | Beginner |
| 0–3 | Not Profitable |

Don't chase the score directly — chase the *behaviors* that raise its components. Consistency (component 3) is the one most traders ignore and the one that most reveals professionalism: eight green weeks in a row is worth more than one explosive week.

### The Emotional Patterns widget

This widget plots your trades by **Emotion Before** and shows average P&L per emotional state. It is, for many traders, the single most valuable screen in the app. The first time you see, in hard numbers, that your "Confident" and "Calm" trades make money and your "Anxious" and "Impatient" trades bleed it — you stop arguing with the idea that psychology is real.

## Chapter 12: The AI Coach & Deep Analysis

The **AI Coach** is the reviewer that never gets tired. It reads your trades, plans, journal entries and emotions *together*. It has two modes.

### Chat mode

Ask anything in plain language. Starter questions the app offers:
- "What's my win rate this month?"
- "Why am I losing on EURUSD?"
- "How do emotions affect my trading?"
- "Suggest strategy improvements"

Ask follow-ups the way you'd ask a mentor: *"Show me my worst day this month and what went wrong,"* or *"Compare my planned vs. unplanned trades."*

### Deep Analysis mode

Pick a focused report:

- **Plan Effectiveness** — your plan-adherence rate, **Planned vs. Unplanned P&L** side by side, an effectiveness score, a list of detected **impulsive trades** (date, symbol, trigger), and recommendations.
- **Sentiment Trends** — whether your emotional state is improving, declining or stable; your dominant emotions with counts; and red flags with severity.
- **Mood Correlation** — your best-performing and worst-performing moods, each with win rate and avg P&L, plus behavioral insights.
- **Trading Patterns** — named patterns with severity, their P&L impact and frequency, the root cause, and a concrete fix — plus a list of your *positive* patterns to keep.

There's also a secondary analysis panel offering **Weekly Summary, Strategy Performance, Emotion Analysis,** and **Risk Assessment**, each shown next to context metrics (total trades, win rate, avg R:R, max drawdown).

### The Floating AI Assistant

The **brain icon** at the bottom-right of every page opens a floating assistant for quick questions without leaving what you're doing. It supports **voice input** and **file upload** — you can drop in a chart screenshot and ask the AI to read it. It can be minimized or expanded full-screen.

### The Proactive Coach & Daily Brief

If you enabled them in onboarding, the coach works *without being asked*:
- **Proactive alerts** fire on real conditions — daily trade limit reached, daily loss limit approaching, a win or loss streak detected, repeated negative emotions, trades taken with no daily plan, profit milestones.
- A **Daily Brief** can greet you each morning with yesterday's performance, today's plan summary, prop-firm status, and one or two focus items — all in under 200 words.

> **How to use the coach well:** don't treat it as an oracle, treat it as a mirror. Its job is to show you what your own data already says. When it tells you something uncomfortable, that's the feature working.

## Chapter 13: Trading Summaries

The **Summaries** page generates AI-written recaps for a **Daily**, **Weekly**, or **Monthly** period. Each summary pulls together your key metrics, your most significant trades, psychological observations, and actionable recommendations.

Build these into your routine:

- **Daily summary** — read it as the last step of your evening loop, right after journaling.
- **Weekly summary** — read it during weekend planning, *before* you write next week's plan. It's the "Review" that feeds the next "Plan."
- **Monthly summary** — pair it with your Hybrid Score and your Plan Correlation alignment score for a complete month-end verdict.

A summary you read and then ignore is worthless. A summary that changes *one line* of next week's plan is the loop closing. Always turn a summary into a concrete change — a new rule, a new checklist item, a removed setup.

# Part V — Risk, Strategy & Discipline

## Chapter 14: Risk Management & Calculators

You cannot journal your way to freedom if a single bad day ends the account. Risk control is survival; survival is what lets compounding happen.

### Risk Management page

AI-assisted portfolio risk analysis: real-time exposure monitoring, volatility-aware stop/target suggestions, a diversification score, and enforcement of daily risk limits.

### Prop Firm Rules tracking

If you trade a funded or challenge account, the **Prop Firm Rules** card tracks **max daily loss ($ and %)** and **trailing drawdown %** against today's live P&L — showing your remaining loss allowance and a violation warning before you blow the rule. Defaults are 5% daily loss and 10% trailing drawdown; set yours to match your firm.

### The Calculators page

| Calculator | Inputs | Output |
|---|---|---|
| **Position Size** | Instrument type, risk % per trade, entry, stop | Suggested size, $ at risk, account impact |
| **Compound** | Start balance, % gain, frequency, period, optional contributions | Future value, total earnings, time-to-double |
| **Expected Value (EV)** | Win probability %, reward, risk | `EV = (P_win × reward) − (P_loss × risk)` |
| **Profit Target** | Risk:reward inputs | Targets by R-multiple |
| **Debt Snowball / Avalanche** | Your debts | A payoff plan |

**Use the Position Size calculator before every trade** — it converts your account-percentage risk into an exact size, so "risk 0.5%" stops being a vibe and becomes a number.

**Use the EV calculator to vet a strategy.** If your setup wins 45% of the time at 2:1, EV per unit risked is `(0.45 × 2) − (0.55 × 1) = +0.35` — positive, keep it. If the math is negative, no amount of journaling will save it; the strategy itself must change.

**Use the Compound calculator for motivation and realism.** It's also a Dashboard widget. Punching in your real numbers reframes the goal: freedom is not one giant trade, it's a survivable edge applied across hundreds of trades. Small, consistent, compounded.

## Chapter 18: Habits of Consistently Profitable Journalers

1. **They log the loss first.** Winners are easy to record. The trader who logs the painful trade *in full, with the honest emotion tag*, is the one who stops repeating it.
2. **They never skip the plan.** Even a 60-second plan beats none. No plan = the AI flags the trade as unplanned, and unplanned trades are where accounts die.
3. **They write the emotion, not the excuse.** "Choppy market" is an excuse. "I was Impatient and Tired" is data.
4. **They review before they plan.** Read yesterday's summary, *then* write today's plan. The loop only works in that order.
5. **They tag consistently.** A small, stable set of tags (`mistake`, `A+`, `revenge-trade`, `news`, `boredom`) turns into searchable analytics. Inventing new tags forever turns into noise.
6. **They chase alignment, not P&L.** A high alignment score with modest profit beats a lucky windfall with a low one — because only the first is repeatable.
7. **They let the AI make them uncomfortable.** When the coach surfaces an ugly pattern, they fix the pattern instead of dismissing the coach.
8. **They protect the streak.** The streak counter is a proxy for the only real edge: showing up and closing the loop, every single day.
